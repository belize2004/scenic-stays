NO. 01 — THE HOUSE

An exercise in transparency, forty steps from the sand.

The Glass House is not merely a residence; it is a lens through which the Gulf of Mexico is perpetually observed. Designed by award-winning architects, the structure utilizes structural glass and bleached oak to dissolve the boundaries between the refined interior and the wild Atlantic coast.

Every floor is a curated experience of light. From the ground-floor garden suite to the soaring third-story observation deck, the materials remain tactile and honest—terrazzo, linen, and aged brass—providing a quiet backdrop for the theatre of the tides.
